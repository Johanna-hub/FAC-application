GATHER

Stand up & be heard

A simple guide to protest in the UK

Protest, organise, change

Public protest is a vital part of British civil, political, economic, social and cultural life. It is the engine that drives positive social change. However, too often, people's efforts are frustrated due to a lack of awareness of their rights and obligations when organising public events.

GATHER will be a resource for people planning marches and demonstrations. Right now, our beta provides information so organisers are sure of the legality of their protest and can stand firm when challenged.

Over the coming months we will be adding further resources to help you plan and complete effective protest as well as the broader context of community organising.

March

Is the planned date of your march after ?

You will need to let the police know at least 6 working days before your planned march. If you are in London, let the Metropolitan police know through their website.

If you live outside of London, contact your local police force and let them know in writing the date and time of the march, the route and the names and addresses of the organisers.

Unfortunately you do not have enough time to notify the police. However, you can hold a static demonstration instead.

Demonstrate

There is no legal requirement to give the police notice for a static demonstration.

If you live in London and would like advice from the police then you can let the police know through their website.

If arranging at short notice, avoid Trafalgar Square and Parliament Square. There are specific rules governing these spaces and you will need to allow enough time to contact the GLA on 0207 983 4750.
